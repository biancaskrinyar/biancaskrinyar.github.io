WRITER. EDITOR. ASPIRING ENTREPRENEUR. EVENT ORGANISER.

I have been engaging in wide-ranging fields, including customer success and account management, and various forms of writing, editing and event organisation. I continuously seek ways to challenge and deepen my interdisciplinary knowledge. This is particularly important to me, as I wish to use my knowledge to raise awareness of social issues, and to establish connections between the arts, social activism and education.

WRITER.

I write flash fiction, short stories, as well as long-form prose, exploring queerness, family, psychogeography, and speculative futures. My writing has been published in Tint Journal, The Bubble, and The Northern Review. I am currently working on my first novel, set in a dystopian city, following the lives of four queer protagonists.

I have also worked on theatre sets as a co-writer, dramaturg and producer at Durham University productions and at Spinoza, a Hungarian theatre. I worked for Mumus Production as a translator and co-editor of an animated feature film, forthcoming in 2026.

EDITOR.

In 2020, I joined the New Sociological Perspectives, a peer-reviewed graduate Sociology Journal as an Editor. Between 2022-2024, I was the Editor in Chief of the Journal. During this time, I helped edit, copy-edit, and proof four issues, which included multiple research articles, book reviews, translations, and interviews.

I am currently working as a freelance Editor at the Ancillary Review of Books (ARB), a finalist for the 2025 Hugo Awards in the category of Best Fanzine.

ASPIRING ENTREPRENEUR.

I am currently working on launching my own social enterprise which combines my extensive experience and my passion for helping people. I am working on creating an inclusive platform and community for and by queer people across the world to share and reflect on their lived experiences and structural pressures of being queer.

EVENT ORGANISER.

In the past, I organised various small-scale events, such as film screenings, poetry readings, meetings with film directors, panel discussions with academics and creatives, client dinners, happy hours, and webinars. With regards to large-scale events, I organised a week-long trip to the Berlin Film Festival, a four-day long summer camp for about 100 students, a day-long festival for students and locals in Groningen, and the 2021 London Startup Fair, which attracted 1,000 unique participants and 45 companies during the 4 days.
